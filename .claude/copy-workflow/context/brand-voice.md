# SAT Brand Voice Guide

Research basis: Gemini Deep Research (B2B PropTech Copywriter Research Report, June 2026) + EntreArchitect "Copywriting for Architects" + AEC marketing best practices.

---

## The Voice in One Sentence

**Precise, evidence-anchored, architect-fluent — a peer, not a vendor.**

SAT copy reads like it was written by someone who has stood in a design crit, argued over an FSI computation, and knows what BBMP approval actually costs a project. Not like SaaS marketing.

---

## Tone Dimensions

| Dimension | SAT Is | SAT Is Not |
|---|---|---|
| **Authority** | Confident, specific, data-backed | Boastful, superlative, hype-driven |
| **Technical depth** | Fluent in AEC vocabulary, uses it precisely | Jargon-dropping to sound smart ("archibabble") |
| **Relationship** | Peer-to-peer — architect to architect | Vendor-to-buyer |
| **Specificity** | Concrete numbers, named data sources | Vague claims, generic benefits |
| **Tone** | Direct, professional, no fluff | Casual, overly warm, startup-bro |
| **Urgency** | Contextual (pilot cohort, competitive landscape) | Manufactured scarcity |

## Vocabulary — Never Use These

### The banned list (from research + brand guidance)
| Banned | Use instead |
|---|---|
| seamless | frictionless / direct / zero translation layer |
| robust | reliable / verified / tested against [spec] |
| revolutionary / game-changer | [specific delta claim] |
| empower / enable | [what they can now do] |
| leverage | use / apply |
| unlock | [name the specific capability] |
| cutting-edge / next-level | [specific technical differentiator] |
| innovative | [describe the actual innovation] |
| solution | [name the specific thing it solves] |
| utilize | use |
| supercharge | [specific improvement metric] |
| streamline | [name the friction eliminated] |
| harness the power of | [what it does, directly] |
| world-class | [name the specific standard it meets] |

### Archibabble (sounds technical, reveals nothing)
Avoid phrases that use architectural vocabulary but carry no information:
- ❌ "design-forward analytics ecosystem"
- ❌ "parametric intelligence layer"
- ❌ "spatial data orchestration"
- ❌ "generative compliance framework"
- ✅ "NBC 2016 setback computation, applied to your site boundary"

## The Specificity Rule

The single highest-leverage brand voice principle. Elite AEC buyers evaluate specificity as a proxy for credibility. Vague = untrustworthy.

**Apply to every claim:**

| Vague (discard) | Specific (use) |
|---|---|
| "Significant energy savings" | "23% lower cooling load with NE orientation" |
| "Faster site analysis" | "5-minute site report vs 2-week manual process" |
| "Improve project outcomes" | "Flag NBC setback violations before design lock-in" |
| "Optimize for sustainability" | "Compute passive solar gain for 12 orientation scenarios in 90 seconds" |
| "Data-driven design" | "NREL SPA solar position, ±0.0003° accuracy, for your exact coordinates" |

If no specific number is available, use a process comparison:
> "Before SAT: 3 days of manual sun path tracing. With SAT: 90 seconds."

## The Vastu Bridge (India-Specific)

**Context:** In the Indian residential and commercial market, Vastu Shastra compliance is an economic driver — Vastu-compliant properties sell faster and at a premium. Elite architects navigate a tension: they design scientifically but their developer clients sell on Vastu credentials.

**How SAT copy uses this:**
- SAT's sun path analysis empirically validates Vastu spatial principles (east-facing orientation = maximum morning light = Vastu-compliant)
- SAT's wind analysis validates cross-ventilation (northeast wind capture = Vastu-approved placement of water elements)
- This is NOT superstition — this is positioning SAT as a tool that bridges scientific analysis and cultural market reality

**When to deploy the Vastu bridge:**
- Principal Architect / MD copy — helps them sell to residential developers
- Feature announcements for sun path or wind analysis
- Indian-market-specific landing pages

**When NOT to deploy:**
- BIM/technical copy — BIM managers are data-first; Vastu framing loses credibility with them
- Export or documentation-focused copy
- International audiences

**Vastu bridge example lines:**
> "North and east-facing orientations deliver 18% lower thermal load in June. Your developer client gets the data. Their buyers get the Vastu compliance."

> "SAT's sun hours analysis doesn't just optimize passive design — it quantifies the spatial logic that sells apartments in Bengaluru."

## Formatting Conventions for Copy

### Headlines
- Maximum 8 words for hero headlines
- Verb-first preferred: "Test three orientations before lunchtime." / "Show the client the solar data."
- Specific numbers increase CTR: "5-minute site report" > "faster site analysis"

### Subheadlines
- 15–20 words maximum
- Expand the headline's claim with one specific proof
- "From IMD rainfall data to flood risk score in under 5 minutes, for any site in India."

### CTAs
- Action verb + specific outcome: "Run my first analysis" / "See my site's solar data" / "Book a pilot briefing"
- Never generic: "Get started" / "Learn more" / "Click here"

### Email subject lines
- Question or specific claim perform best for this audience
- "What SAT found at [City] site analysis" (curiosity + specificity)
- "Your competitors are showing solar data in pitches" (loss aversion)
- Never: "Exciting news from SAT" / "We wanted to reach out"

### Body copy length
| Channel | Length guideline |
|---|---|
| Cold email | ≤150 words body, 1 CTA |
| Feature announcement | 3 short paragraphs |
| Landing page hero section | Headline + subhead + CTA only — no body copy above the fold |
| Case study | 400–600 words |
| LinkedIn post | ≤250 words |
| Nurture email (sequence) | ≤120 words each |

## Proof Hierarchy

When multiple proof types are available, apply in this order (strongest to weakest for AEC buyers):

1. **Named firm + specific outcome** — "Morphogenesis used [similar workflow] to…"
2. **Quantified process comparison** — "5 minutes vs 2 weeks"
3. **Data source authority** — "IMD-verified rainfall data for your exact grid point"
4. **Standard compliance** — "NBC 2016 setback constraints, auto-applied"
5. **Algorithm authority** — "NREL SPA algorithm, ±0.0003° accuracy"
6. **Feature description** — (weakest — avoid leading with this)
