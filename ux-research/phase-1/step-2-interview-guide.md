# Interview Guide — Step 2
# SAT UX Research — Phase 1
# Version 1.0 — 2026-06-10

**Total target duration:** 45–60 min
**Format:** Remote video call, recorded with consent
**Profiles covered:** Junior Architect · Senior Architect · Urban Planner / M.Arch Student
**Note:** Guide reflects questions actually used in 2026-06-09 session (P01, P02) and SME-derived questions for synthetic profiles (P03–P05).

## Pre-interview checklist

- [ ] Recording consent confirmed verbally: *"We're recording this session for internal research only. It won't be shared externally. Are you okay with that?"*
- [ ] Framing set: *"We're not testing you — we're learning from you. There are no right answers."*
- [ ] Participant confirmed name, role, firm (or institution), city
- [ ] Note-taking format: behaviour > opinion; quote verbatim when strong reaction
- [ ] Show-me prompt ready: *"Could you share your screen and show me a recent site analysis report or a tool you use?"*

---

## Section 1 — Warm-up `[5 min]`

**Goal:** Establish participant context; build rapport; confirm profile match.

**Q1.** Tell me about your current role and the type of projects you've been working on in the last 12 months.

**Q2.** How long have you been personally responsible for site analysis tasks — even partially — whether in practice or in your final-year projects?

---

## Section 2 — Workflow `[10 min]`

**Goal:** Understand day-to-day process at a high level before diving into pain points.

[SAT-PRIORITY]
**Q3.** Walk me through your typical site analysis process from start to finish — from first receiving a project brief to when you consider site analysis complete. What are the key stages? What are the most challenging parts? How long does it take? What data sources are critical? How do you deal with data inaccuracy?

*Probes if needed:*
- *What are the first two or three things you do when you get a new site?*
- *When do you consider site analysis done — what does "done" look like?*
- *How does a firm handle this differently from a student doing it alone?*

---

## Section 3 — Site analysis specifics `[15 min]`

**Goal:** Surface tool frustrations, data gaps, and workflow integration points.

[SAT-PRIORITY]
**Q4.** What kind of software tools or apps do you currently use for site modelling or analysis? *(AutoCAD, SketchUp, QGIS, Revit, Rhino/Grasshopper, online tools — anything)*

**Q5.** Where do you get site data for sun angles, wind direction, flood risk, climate, and zoning? What are your go-to sources? How reliable are they?

[SAT-PRIORITY]
**Q6.** What is the biggest manual task or data gap that your software does not handle at all — where do you end up doing things by hand, guessing, or just skipping?

**Q7.** What frustrates you the most about site analysis today? What costs the most time?

**Q8.** Which part of the site analysis process do you find most confusing, uncertain, or time-consuming?

**Q9.** How do you handle Indian regulatory requirements — NBC 2016, local DCR, setbacks, FSI? How do you look things up? How authoritative do the sources feel?

**Q10.** Have you ever been told your site analysis was wrong or missing something important? What happened?

---

## Section 4 — Ideal tool `[10 min]`

**Goal:** Elicit unprompted product vision; validate SAT direction.

[SAT-PRIORITY]
**Q11.** If you could see one live-updated dashboard of site data while doing your analysis — imagine you've just logged in and the site is on screen — what information would you want to see? What would you keep coming back to?

[SAT-PRIORITY]
**Q12.** What is the most ideal version of a site analysis tool built specifically for Indian practice? What would it do that nothing today does?

*Show-me probe (if applicable):*
- *Can you show me the tool or document you use most during site analysis right now?*

---

## Section 5 — Close `[5 min]`

**Goal:** Capture anything missed; gauge prototype testing interest; get referrals.

**Q13.** Is there anything about your site analysis process we haven't covered that you think is important?

**Q14.** Would you be willing to test an early prototype of SAT in a future session and give us feedback?

**Q15.** Do you know any other architects, urban planners, or students who would be good candidates for this research? *(Senior architects especially needed)*
